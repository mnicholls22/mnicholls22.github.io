Letter Overhaul

This was a problem nobody could solve. Millions of system-generated letters were sent to members every year, but nobody could provide an inventory of what they were. The letters sent out were lacking empathy and difficult to understand. Many times, these would land on the CEO's desk. This was a problem in a company with an ever-increasing focus on consumer centricity.

This project started out as a fact-finding mission to find out where these letters were coming from, but evolved into much more.

My role was multi-faceted. I led the charge to establish a cross-functional team to document and inventory the letter landscape, eventually identifying 2,100+ consumer letters. From there, I spearheaded efforts to humanize our letters using plain language (and a lot of common sense). Consumer research validated our approach and I set out to deploy those communications from several core technology platforms. In the process, we re-engineered our triggering and composition process - laying the groundwork for future efficiencies and electronic delivery.

Communication Dashboard

I shifted into a new role, one that provides unbiased measurement and reporting on post-enrollment campaigns and their effectiveness. Previously, there was little-to-no collection and sharing of this info.

After meeting with key players and learning about current state, an opportunity arose to radically increase transparency via a communications dashboard.

I created a process to collect and transform information across several data sources to stand up this interactive online tool. Then I created filterable views for key measures, such as email & paperless opt-in, web and telehealth registration, mobile and web portal use, email engagement, spend, and other communication metrics.

Paperless Initiative

Quantifying and delivering hard dollar savings wasn't a strong cultural trait for the team. But with an enterprise focus on organizational efficiency, Marketing needed to up their game to demonstrate their bottom-line impact.

I was able to step in and help lead an initiative focused on increasing paperless opt-in, providing much needed structure and rigor to the approach. I consolated assumptions, expenses, savings projections, and other data points into a business case. After fleshing out internally, we collaborated with other business owners to align on our methodology. Ultimately, this earned us a significant investment and helped convince cost center owners to give up impending savings.
